02

Why you stop noticing it

Glasses bother you at a few specific spots — the same ones that hurt when you lie down. Dozi softens each one on purpose to stay below the point where you'd feel it. Tap a zone.

Zone 1 · Nose bridge

A saddle, not pads.

Traditional nose pads concentrate the whole frame's weight onto ~1.5–2cm² — creating 100–300 g/cm² of pressure. Dozi uses a single continuous silicone saddle that spreads contact across 8–12cm²: four to six times the area, a fraction of the pressure. The soft 30–35A silicone conforms to bridge heights from flat to prominent — fitting faces conventional frames don't.

8–12cm² contact area · 4–6× less pressure
Zone 2 · Temporal region

Retention without clamping.

Above 0.90N of clamping force, temples cause discomfort within 30 minutes; above 1.20N, headaches by 60. With no hinge to clamp, Dozi holds itself with the silicone's own gentle spring tension — an oval-profile arm tuned to stay at or below 0.50N. It grips like swim goggles, without a strap, without a pinch.

≤0.50N target · vs 0.90N discomfort threshold
Zone 3 · Brow band

Rests light on your brow

Because Dozi wraps around, the top of the frame settles softly on your brow bone — sharing the weight so it doesn't all land on your nose. We keep that contact barely-there, line it in cooling silk where it meets your skin, and tilt the lens slightly downward so reading in bed feels natural.

Barely-there pressure · silk-lined · tilted for reading
Behind the ear

No hard tip, ever.

The longest-duration pain point in normal glasses is the hard temple tip behind the ear. Dozi's tip is continuous soft silicone — a gentle 140–160° sweep over a large 15×8mm surface, softened to 35–40A and fully silk-lined to eliminate friction. Nothing digs in, even lying on your side.

15×8mm surface · 35–40A tip · silk-lined

The fog & heat problem

A close wrap creates a warm microclimate. Dozi vents it with indirect micro-channels (1.5–2mm) at the top of the lens carrier — above the optical zone, angled to stop drafts — plus a permanent hydrophilic anti-fog layer that spreads moisture into an invisible film instead of beading it.

  • Upper-only venting keeps stray light out during TV
  • Hydrophilic anti-fog bonded at the factory, never sprayed
  • Silk wicks moisture away from the lens rim

Sleep science

Blue light, blocked where it matters.

Light in the 460–480nm range activates the melanopsin cells in your retina that suppress melatonin. Block those wavelengths in the 2–3 hours before bed and melatonin rises naturally. Blue-light filtering is standard on every Dozi — and you choose how far to take it.

Clear

Premium AR coating, blocks 20–30% at 415–455nm. Near-invisible tint for all-day wear.

Amber

Embedded pigment blocks 75–85% of 400–500nm. For evening screen time, 2–3 hours pre-sleep.

Red-orange

Blocks ~100% of blue and green to 550nm. Maximum melatonin protection for the final hour.

Built for every nose

The single biggest fit failure in eyewear is nasal bridge variation — flat (East Asian average 8–10mm), medium (10–13mm), and high (Caucasian average 12–16mm). A rigid bridge can only fit one. Dozi's wide silicone saddle (26–30mm) with soft 30–35A deformation conforms across the entire 8–16mm range. Two sizes cover ~90% of adults.
